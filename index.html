Forecasting
Belief propagation across the book
If rates, a sponsor, a sector, a borrower metric, or a covenant outcome changes, the system estimates what else should move and ranks implications by book-level importance.
Compounding
Posttraining from Apollo decisions
The agent system creates the training substrate: underwriting priors, live posteriors, human overrides, realized outcomes, calibration scores, and deal-level decision quality.
What Prior Axion Work Contributes
Capital-Allocator Pattern
Move from flat research to an insight surface
The reusable pattern is not a report. It is a surface that combines public prior, private posterior, market view, and graph impact into a ranked stream of actionable deltas.
Fusion Pattern
Disagreement is signal
Different agents reason from different decompositions. In credit, debt-capacity, sponsor-behavior, sector, covenant, and liquidity agents should disagree visibly before a human decides.
Forecaster Quality Pattern
The objective is calibration under uncertainty
Apollo-specific models should be judged by Brier score, stability, robustness to framing, coherent probabilities, and realized decision quality, not by how convincingly they write memos.
The Jane Street analogy is cultural, not cosmetic: every position is marked, every decision is decomposed into probabilities, every disagreement is routed, and every outcome improves the machinery.
The End State
Machine-Native Private Credit
Apollo can become the institution that knows, continuously, who should own each private-credit risk and at what price.
The initial wedge is surveillance plus origination triage. The compounding advantage is an Apollo-specific posttrained credit model. The capstone is a liquidity layer: risk transfer, internal transfer pricing, secondary sale recommendations, and ultimately a new market structure around originated private credit.
The main product object should be the Apollo Credit Surface: a live, queryable map of what Apollo believed at origination, what it should believe now, what markets imply, and what the rest of the book should infer.

The first system is an agent network, not a single autonomous underwriter. It combines specialized credit agents, explicit routing, permissioned human review, and an audit trail for every belief update.
Agent Families
01
Surveillance agents
Monitor borrower updates, covenants, filings, macro, sector news, payment behavior, collateral data, and sponsor actions. Output: changed beliefs and affected exposures.
02
Origination agents
Ingest a new lending request, map comparable Apollo deals, generate base/downside cases, identify missing diligence, and recommend whether to pursue.
03
Propagation agents
Take a change in one node and estimate the rest of the book: linked sponsors, sectors, covenants, funds, refi walls, and liquidity channels.
04
Manager-extension agents
Represent the questions and operating style of credit leaders: what they ask first, what they mistrust, what exceptions matter, and when they want escalation.
05
Agent-to-agent comms
A request from a manager routes to relevant agents and internal people. Draft agent responses can be reviewed, corrected, approved, or blocked before propagation.
06
Liquidity agents
Evaluate internal transfer pricing, risk-transfer trades, secondary appetite, structuring alternatives, buyer fit, and liability-aware ownership.

Design Principle
Disagreement Routing
The system should not hide disagreement behind a single recommendation.
For a material credit, one agent may reason from debt capacity, another from sponsor behavior, another from sector conditions, another from covenant math, and another from liquidity. When those agents disagree, the output is a ranked inspection path for Apollo humans.
Consensus probability Agent spread Evidence gaps Escalation triggers
The roadmap should be staged around compounding data rights and operational trust. Start with a bounded book. Earn the right to touch origination. Then use the trace data to posttrain Apollo-specific credit judgment.

Bogdan's three buckets map cleanly to one compounding system. The agent system creates the traces. Posttraining turns traces into Apollo-specific behavior. Pretraining only becomes sensible once the corpus and labels are strong enough.
Agent System -> Posttraining -> Pretraining
Layer 1

Agent system

Continuously monitor, triage, reason, propagate, route, and write auditable decision traces. This is the first product and the data-generating machine.

Layer 2

Posttraining

Use Apollo memos, outcomes, overrides, and scored forecasts to train agents that are calibrated for Apollo's domains, risk appetite, language, and escalation rules.

Layer 3

Pretraining / continued pretraining

Once data is normalized and outcome labels exist, train a deeper Apollo private-credit model that has internalized sponsor behavior, covenant dynamics, recovery patterns, and origination context.

Training Objective
Do not train a better memo writer. Train calibrated credit judgment.
The benchmark suite should measure calibration, stability, robustness to framing, probability coherence, responsiveness to new evidence, sharpness, and decision quality. A model that writes beautifully but misses covenant breaches is a failure.
